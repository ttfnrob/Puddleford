⏳ Historical Timeline

Puddleford exists across time. The same town, different eras - always surprising.

1695
"Captain Breadbeard"
Pirates descend on Puddleford. Captain Breadbeard and his crew terrorise the townsfolk, though quite what they hoped to plunder from a small English village remains a matter of historical debate.
1790s
Early Series Episodes
The Georgian era visits to Puddleford. Corsets, candlelight and considerable scandal.
1830s
"The Hunchback of Puddleford"
A Victorian-era tale loosely inspired by a famous novel. Puddleford's version has its own distinct charm and architectural peculiarities.
1930s
Early Series Episodes
Puddleford between the wars. Tweed, tension, and the ever-present Dog and Duck.
1970s
"Faster and Furious"
High-octane thrills arrive in Puddleford. Whether the cobbled streets were entirely suitable for the events that followed is open to question.
1983
A Very Specific Year
Something specific happened in 1983. The Players were very clear about the year. The details remain classified.
1990s
"The Pandora Strain"
Something was released in Puddleford that perhaps should not have been. The nineties were a complicated time.
2000s
"Pressed for Love"
Romance in the new millennium. Puddleford's complicated relationship with modern technology and matters of the heart.
Present Day
"The Eye of Puddleford"
Puddleford today. Some things have changed. The Dog and Duck has not.
2180s
"Where No Duck Has Gone Before"
The far future. Puddleford reaches for the stars - or the stars come to Puddleford, which given the UFO situation may not be unprecedented.
Puddlefordia
Fantasy Realm
A parallel fantasy realm version of Puddleford, where the town's quirks and characters are refracted through an entirely magical lens. Dragons. Probably.
The Wild West
"New Puddleford"
The frontier. Someone took Puddleford west. The cobblestones did not survive the journey but the spirit - and apparently the pub - did.

Recurring Locations

Some places in Puddleford endure across centuries. Whether by magic, stubbornness, or an unusually robust planning permission, they persist.

The Dog and Duck
The heart of Puddleford across every era. The ale changes. The clientele changes. The inexplicable back room does not. If you need to find someone in Puddleford, they are probably here.
Puddleford Bridge
The cobbled bridge over the river. Appears in nearly every episode. Has witnessed duels, declarations of love, suspicious exchanges, and at least one incident involving a cart of fish.
️
Puddleford Manor
The stately home on the hill overlooking the town. Ownership has changed many times. Its reputation for strange events has not.
Puddleford General Hospital
The irrational health service. Medical procedures of questionable logic, staff of uncertain qualification, and a waiting room that seems to exist outside of normal time.
