The wiki grows with each episode. All details are entirely improvised on the spot by the Puddleford Players — so the history of Puddleford is always subject to revision, contradiction, and the occasional UFO.

Through the Ages

Puddleford has existed across many periods of history — and beyond. Here are the eras visited so far.

1695
"Captain Breadbeard"
Pirates arrive in Puddleford. The sea is not nearby. This has not deterred them.
1790s
Series One — early episodes
Late Georgian Puddleford. Bonnets, schemes, and considerable scandal.
1830s
"The Hunchback of Puddleford"
Victorian drama in Puddleford. Someone has a very dramatic back.
1930s
Series One — early episodes
The interwar years come to Puddleford. Jazz, intrigue, and questionable decisions.
1970s
"Faster and Furious"
Cars, flares, and something going very fast through Puddleford.
1983
A very specific year
Not the 1980s in general. 1983 in particular. The distinction matters enormously.
1990s
"The Pandora Strain"
Something has been released in Puddleford. Best not to ask too many questions.
2000s
"Pressed for Love"
The millennium has arrived. Puddleford adapts, imperfectly.
Present Day
"The Eye of Puddleford"
Something is watching. Puddleford carries on regardless.
2180s
"Where No Duck Has Gone Before"
The far future. Puddleford has, somehow, made it to space. The ducks have come too.
The Wild West
"New Puddleford"
A frontier town that bears a suspicious resemblance to Puddleford. Cowboys, saloons, and a mysterious piano player.
Puddlefordia
"The Fellowship of the Dog and Duck"
A fantasy realm. The Dog and Duck still exists. The dark times are remembered by no one, which is convenient.

Recurring Locations

Certain places in Puddleford have endured across the centuries. These are their stories.

🍺

The Dog and Duck

Puddleford's enduring public house. Present in every era — from Puddlefordia's fantasy realm to the 23rd century. The ale is always questionable. The company varies.

🌉

Puddleford Bridge

The cobbled stone bridge at the heart of town, spanning the River Pudd. Has been the site of confrontations, declarations of love, and at least one incident involving a goat.

🏛️

Puddleford Manor

The grand stately home on the hill. Seat of the local gentry. Has hosted balls, investigations, and a rather inconvenient fountain.

🏥

Puddleford General Hospital

Also known as the Irrational Health Service. Staffed by devoted professionals facing impossible odds and sky-high jacuzzi prices from the rival private clinic.

📞

The Red Phone Box

A classic British telephone box, visible in the town centre across many eras. Nobody is entirely sure who it belongs to or why it's sometimes glowing.

🌾

The Fortiesqueue Farm

A working farm on the outskirts of Puddleford, run by simple farmers. Recently entangled in a corporate conspiracy involving contaminated cows.
