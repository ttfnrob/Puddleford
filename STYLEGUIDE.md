# Puddleford Style Guide

House style for all Puddleford content: the website, social media, show notes, episode descriptions, and wiki entries.

## Voice and tone

- Warm, understated British wit. Not trying too hard. Fond of the town and its inhabitants.
- Dry rather than wacky. Confident rather than breathless.
- Treat Puddleford as a real place with a real history, even though everything is made up.
- Never use marketing speak or hype. Let the content speak for itself.

## Grammar and punctuation

- British English throughout (favourite not favorite, colour not color, realise not realize, etc).
- No em-dashes. Not the character (—), not a double hyphen (--). Use a comma, a full stop, or restructure the sentence.
- No exclamation marks except in direct speech or episode titles that already contain one.
- Oxford comma: yes.
- Numbers: spell out one to nine, numerals from 10 onwards. Years always as numerals (1983, the 1790s).
- Dates: day month year, no ordinals. Write 10 May 2026, not May 10th 2026.
- Ellipsis: avoid. If a thought trails off, end it properly.

## Specific words and phrases

- "entirely improvised" not "fully improvised" or "completely improvised".
- "the Puddleford Players" with a lowercase "the" and capital P on Players.
- "MKTOC" always in caps. Spell out as "Milton Keynes Theatre of Comedy" on first mention per page.
- Refer to episodes by title in quotes: "Captain Breadbeard".
- Historical periods: "the 1790s", "the present day" (lowercase), "the Wild West" (capitalised as a proper noun).
- "transcript" not "transcription".

## What to avoid

- No emoji in body content. The nav logo may use a small decorative mark if appropriate, but keep it minimal.
- No em-dashes, anywhere.
- No exclamation marks in body copy.
- No "amazing", "incredible", "unique", "journey", "dive into", "explore".
- No invented facts about the players or episodes. If you do not know something for certain, do not write it.
